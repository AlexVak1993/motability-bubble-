Top Motability 4X4s

If you have limited mobility, there’s no reason why you shouldn’t have access to a practical, easy-to-drive car that looks good too. 4x4 SUVs have become very popular on the Motability Scheme thanks to the many features that make them stylish accessories and excellent road companions for everyone, not just those who need a little help to get out and about.

Many Motability 4x4s have a higher driving position which allows better visibility and easy access. Spacious luggage capacity is ideal for transporting wheelchairs and walking aids while easy handling can be had thanks to their compact size and 4x4 properties.

The Motability Scheme makes car ownership ever so easy with the inclusion of relevant adaptations, annual Road Tax, breakdown cover, some servicing, maintenance and repairs at no extra cost. Let’s take a look at some of the most popular Motability 4x4s currently available on the scheme. Choose any one of these and you could be enjoying a more comfortable and accessible drive in no time...

Audi

Q2

When it comes to Motability 4x4 capability the Audi Q2, the Q range’s smallest SUV, is quite particular. Only the most powerful automatic engines offer the famed Audi quattro all-wheel-drive. However, with a choice of 4 different trim levels and a range of colours, the options are anything but limited.

Manual seat height adjustment is standard on all models and lumbar support is an optional extra. As with all compact SUVs, what you gain in handling and parkability, you’ll sacrifice in the interior space, meaning that the rear seats could feel snug for 3 and the small door width makes access a little tight. Nevertheless, a boot capacity of 405 litres, and more than twice that with the rear seats down, is still good.

Citroën

C5 Aircross

Huge front seats and one of the best suspensions in its class allow for exceptional comfort in the Citroën C5 Aircross. With its quirky looks, including Airbump door and wheel arch protectors, this Motability 4x4 proves that practical doesn’t have to be boring. The large front space does mean that rear legroom is a tad limited but the sliding and reclining back seats, and a 580 litre boot space (even more with the rear seats down) go some way to compensate.

Keyless entry, park assist and electric parking brake all make driving easier, especially if your mobility is limited. An 8'' touchscreen allows users to control temperature, multimedia, navigation and phone (including wireless charging), all from one place.

Volkswagen

Tiguan

WAnother great looking Motability 4x4, the Volkswagen Tiguan is certainly eye-catching, with its curvy silhouette, tilt and slide panoramic sunroof, tinted rear windows and up to 18-inch alloy wheels. The luxurious interior offers an ergonomic layout and flexible seating with sophisticated design features, such as ambient lighting and contemporary upholstery.

A multifunction steering wheel and 8-inch colour touchscreen put everything at your fingertips while a level boot area with variable floor height and load through facility makes loading and unloading as easy on your back as the VW TIguan is on the eye. 4MOTION Active Control provides four different driving modes - On road, Off-road, Off-road Individual and Snow - to suit every condition.

Honda

CR-V Hybrid

Anyone who loves their gadgets or hankers after an easy life will enjoy the Honda CR-V Hybrid. Not only does it make use of the latest hybrid self-charging technology which doesn’t require plug in, this Motability 4x4 boasts not one but two 7-inch screens - one for configuration, the other for infotainment.

Climate control and parking sensors will keep you cool in any situation, as will the CVT automatic gearbox which is paired with all-wheel-drive capability for even easier handling. The CR-V’s innovative safety features - Honda Sensing - give you the peace of mind you need to provide you and your loved ones with some invaluable extra freedom.

Jeep

Renegade

While it might not look like your typical Motability 4x4, the boxy shape of the Jeep Renegade actually makes it very practical indeed. Doors open to a very accommodating 70 degrees (front) or 80 degrees (rear), and with the back seats folded can provide a cavernous 1,297 litres of luggage, wheelchair or equipment space.

Cruise control, lane departure warning and traffic sign recognition allow you to relax a little while in charge of the wheel while the hard-wearing mesh fabric of the seats are a match for any transfer plate. 5 different drive modes, including ‘snow’ and ‘mud’, and a 9-speed automatic transmission mean that you can get pretty much anywhere.
